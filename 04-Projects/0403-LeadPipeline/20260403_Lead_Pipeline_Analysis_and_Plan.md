## Your Pipeline — Rebuilt Right

Here's the framework to organize your 360 contacts into a real, workable pipeline:

### Tier 1: Hot (Target: 10–20 contacts)
Active buyers/sellers in a 30–90 day transaction window. Pre-approved, actively touring, or about to list. You are in regular contact (weekly minimum). These contacts should have calendar follow-ups and open tasks in BoldTrail.

### Tier 2: Warm / Watch (Target: 30–60 contacts)
Real people, real interest, but 90–180 day window. Maybe they need to sell first, waiting on a life event, or still casually browsing. Monthly touchpoint. These are your "coming soon" pipeline.

### Tier 3: Nurture (Target: 50–100 contacts)
6+ month window or unverified intent. Active enough to keep, not ready enough to chase. Smart drip campaign in BoldTrail handles them automatically. Quarterly check-in.

### Tier 4: Past Client Re-Engagement (235 contacts)
These don't live in your active pipeline — they live in a dedicated re-engagement track. See plan below.

### Tier 5: Archive / Triage Later
Contacts with no email, no phone, no activity, and no source. Pull them to a list and decide individually.

## The 4-Week Action Plan

### Week 1 — Hot Lead Triage (The Big Cleanup)
**Goal:** Get your "Hot" count from 111 down to real Tier 1 pipeline (10–20).

Work through the 111 Hot contacts in BoldTrail. For each:

1. **Check last activity date.** If they've been Hot since 2022 or 2023 and haven't visited your site or responded, they are not hot.
2. **Ask yourself:** "Did I actually talk to this person recently? Are they actively looking?" Yes = keep Hot. No = downgrade.
3. **Downgrade options:**
   - Spoke with them, 3–6 month window → move to **Watch**
   - Valid lead, just not ready → move to **Nurture**
   - No response to 3+ attempts, old lead → move to **Cold** or **Archive**
   - Never heard from them at all → attempt one re-engagement email/text, then move to Nurture or Cold

**Target by end of Week 1:** Hot list trimmed to 15–25 verified, active leads.

---

### Week 2 — Past Client Re-Engagement Campaign
**Goal:** Activate your 235 past clients as a referral engine.

**Step 1:** Segment your past clients into three buckets:
- Bucket A: Bought/sold in the last 3 years — high re-engagement potential
- Bucket B: 3–7 years ago — referral potential, check in
- Bucket C: 7+ years ago, no recent activity — add to annual touchpoint list only

**Step 2:** Build a BoldTrail Smart Campaign for past clients:
- **Month 1:** "Just checking in" personal email/text — no pitch, just relationship
- **Month 3:** Market update email for their neighborhood
- **Month 6:** Home value update / "Did you know your home is worth X now?" hook
- **Month 9:** Referral ask — "Do you know anyone thinking about buying or selling?"
- **Month 12:** Anniversary of their closing — handwritten note or card

**Step 3:** Pull your highest-rated past clients (anyone you know well) and call them personally this week. 10 calls. No ask — just reconnect.

### Week 3 — Source Cleanup & Lead Scoring
**Goal:** Fix the source fragmentation and start scoring every active lead.

**Source cleanup (bulk edit in BoldTrail):**
- Merge REFINDLY + REfindley → `Refindly`
- Merge GOOGLE + google → `Google-Organic`
- Merge ADWORDS → `Google-Paid`
- Merge DIRECT + Direct Website → `Direct`
- Merge FB_TeamListingAds → `Facebook-Ads`
- Merge Friends and Family + FRIENDORFAMILY → `Friends-and-Family`

**Lead scoring (5 minutes per day):**
Go through your trimmed Hot list and rate every contact 1–5 stars in BoldTrail:
- 5 stars: Pre-approved, actively touring, strong communication
- 4 stars: Verified intent, good communication, near-ready
- 3 stars: Real person, real interest, 90–180 day window
- 2 stars: Unverified intent, minimal communication
- 1 star: Old lead, cold, keep for long-term nurture only

---

### Week 4 — Build the Referral Machine
**Goal:** Create a system that generates referral leads on autopilot.

**1. Identify your referral champions.** These are past clients who love you, who are active on social media, and who have referred before (or said they would). Aim for a list of 20–30 people. Add a "referral-champion" tag in BoldTrail.

**2. Create a Referral Campaign in BoldTrail:**
- Quarterly personal outreach (text or call — not bulk email)
- Birthday/anniversary recognition
- Market updates that make them look smart when they share them with friends

**3. Ask directly.** The most powerful referral generation is the simplest: after any positive interaction with a past client, ask — "I'm always looking to help great people like you. If you know anyone thinking about buying or selling in the Naples/Fort Myers area, I'd love an introduction."

**4. Build relationships with referral partners:**
- 1–2 local mortgage lenders who will send you pre-approved buyers
- 1 estate attorney for inheritance/probate-related listings
- 1 financial advisor in the snowbird/retiree demographic
- 1 relocation coordinator at a major local employer

## Ongoing Weekly Maintenance System

Once you've completed the 4-week build, this is the weekly cadence to keep your pipeline healthy:

### Monday (15 min) — Pipeline Review
- Open BoldTrail Lead Pipeline view
- Review all Hot contacts: Does each one have a next follow-up task set?
- Any new leads that came in over the weekend? Assign status + rating immediately
- Check for contacts with overdue tasks

### Wednesday (20 min) — Nurture Touch
- Pick 5 Nurture contacts and send a personal check-in text or email (not from a drip — from you)
- Review any BoldTrail Smart Campaign bounce/unsubscribe reports

### Friday (10 min) — Weekly Tally
- Count of Hot leads (should stay 10–25)
- Count of new leads this week + their sources
- Any deals that progressed? Update status in BoldTrail
- Log anything significant in your daily note

### Monthly (30 min) — Deep Review
- Review all Watch contacts: anything ready to move to Hot?
- Review all Nurture contacts: any that went silent or asked to unsubscribe?
- Check source performance: which sources sent the best leads this month?
- Reconnect with 5 past clients personally (call or handwritten note)
